About Me

The Long Story (AKA, How did I get here?)

As I mentioned, I'm working on a master's degree in software development. However, I am probably not what you picture when you imagine a STEM student.

I spent the first fifteen or so years of my career doing tradeswork - residential construction and facilities maintenance mostly, but I've done a little bit of everything in and around a job site.

None of this was my dream career.

In fact, I've pretty much always known that I wanted to be a software engineer, but life, as it frequently does, had other plans. A few years ago I decided to go back to school to try to make that dream a reality. My academic career to that point was unremarkable, so a proper computer science program was out of the question. Still, I believed I could get there, even if I had to take the roundabout way to do it. After three years of hard work, I completed a Bachelor of Multidisciplinary Studies degree at West Virginia University, graduating with a 4.0 GPA. My final year, I taught myself to code in Python and built a small portfolio of projects. Soon after, I was accepted into The University of Pennsylvania's Master of Computer and Information Technology program. Touted as a CS program for those without CS background, MCIT was my first real introduction to the world of programming and computer science.

But, again, life had other plans.

A few weeks into my first semester at UPenn, my two-year-old son was diagnosed with acute lymphblastic leukemia - ALL, for short. As devastating as the news was, we were fortunate: ALL is extremely treatable. It did, however, mean a nearly three-year treatment period, consisting of weekly (and eventually at-home) chemotherapy treatments. I took a formal leave of absence from school, and taking care of my son became my full-time job.

Six months into my son's treatment, I returned to school part-time, splitting my time between caring for him and trying to keep up with UPenn's demanding expectations. I did that for two years, and made it through the core CS classes, maintaining a 3.72 GPA. But I was stressed to the breaking point. After a second leave of absence due to my son being hospitalized for a bacterial infection, I knew that didn't want to return to UPenn. I wanted more practical work, and less theory, to be challenged without feeling like I wanted to jump off a bridge around mid-terms. So I began looking at software development/engineering degrees instead.

"Perseverance is the steadfast pursuit of an objective despite obstacles, difficulties or discouragement." - Albert Schweitzer

A few months ago, my son completed his treatment, and I transferred to Boston University into the MSSD program. Aside from BU's excellent reputation, I was impressed by their curriculum - classes in web development, mobile applications, agile methodology. All things that would be invaluable to a career-changer like myself. Although this is my first semester, I feel very strongly that I made a great choice with BU. I know that the skills I'm learning will have immediate transfer, letting me hit the ground running when I find my first position. And while the University of Pennsylvania is obviously a great school, I know that it wasn't right for me at this time in my life.

Although I know that I have a lot to learn, and that the learning journey will never truly stop, I believe that I have the skills, drive, and curiosity to be a great software engineer. If you'd like to join me on this journey, please check out my blog, where I plan to document everything, or the projects section where I highlight what I've built or am currently working on.
